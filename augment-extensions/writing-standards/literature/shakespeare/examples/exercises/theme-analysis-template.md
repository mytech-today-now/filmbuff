## Example: "Appearance vs. Reality" in Hamlet

For a completed example of this template, see how it might be filled out for the theme of "Appearance vs. Reality" in *Hamlet*:

**Play Title**: Hamlet
**Genre**: ☑ Tragedy
**Primary Theme**: Appearance vs. Reality

**One-Sentence Description**:
*Hamlet* explores how things are not what they seem, from Claudius's false kingship to Hamlet's feigned madness, questioning the nature of truth and deception.

**Key Quotes**:
1. "Seems, madam? Nay, it is. I know not 'seems.'" (Hamlet, Act 1, Scene 2)
   - Hamlet distinguishes between appearance and genuine feeling

2. "One may smile, and smile, and be a villain" (Hamlet, Act 1, Scene 5)
   - Hamlet realizes Claudius's friendly exterior hides his murderous nature

3. "I am but mad north-north-west. When the wind is southerly, I know a hawk from a handsaw." (Hamlet, Act 2, Scene 2)
   - Hamlet hints his madness is an act, not reality

**Characters Embodying Theme**:
- **Claudius**: Appears to be a legitimate king but is actually a murderer and usurper
- **Hamlet**: Feigns madness to hide his true intentions
- **Polonius**: Appears wise but is actually foolish and meddling
- **Ophelia**: Her madness is real, contrasting with Hamlet's feigned madness

**Imagery & Symbolism**:
- **Masks/Acting**: The play-within-a-play ("The Mousetrap") reveals hidden truth
- **Poison**: Hidden in the ear (King Hamlet's murder) - truth hidden beneath surface
- **Mirrors**: "Hold the mirror up to nature" - theater reveals reality

**Modern Relevance**:
This theme resonates today with social media personas, political spin, fake news, and the difficulty of discerning truth from performance in modern life.
