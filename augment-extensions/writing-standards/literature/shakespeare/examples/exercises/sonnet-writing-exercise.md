# Sonnet Writing Exercise

## Overview

This exercise guides you through writing a complete Shakespearean sonnet, from initial concept to polished final draft.

**Time Required**: 1-2 hours
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced
**Prerequisites**: Understanding of iambic pentameter and rhyme schemes

## The Shakespearean Sonnet Form

### Structure
- **14 lines** total
- **Three quatrains** (4 lines each) + **one couplet** (2 lines)
- **Rhyme scheme**: ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
- **Meter**: Iambic pentameter (10 syllables per line, unstressed/stressed pattern)

### Thematic Structure
- **Quatrain 1** (lines 1-4): Introduce topic/problem/question
- **Quatrain 2** (lines 5-8): Develop or complicate the topic
- **Quatrain 3** (lines 9-12): Volta (turn) - shift in perspective or argument
- **Couplet** (lines 13-14): Resolution, conclusion, or twist

## Step 1: Choose Your Topic

### Suggested Topics
1. **Time and Change**: Seasons, aging, memory, loss
2. **Love**: Romantic, familial, unrequited, enduring
3. **Nature**: Specific natural phenomena as metaphor
4. **Art and Immortality**: Poetry's power to preserve
5. **Paradox**: Contradictory truths (love/hate, joy/sorrow)

## Step 5: Check and Adjust Meter

### Iambic Pentameter Pattern
```
u / | u / | u / | u / | u /
(10 syllables: unstressed/stressed pairs)
```

### Scan Each Line

For each line, mark the stressed syllables and count:
- **10 syllables?** (or 11 with feminine ending)
- **Unstressed/stressed pattern?**
- **Natural speech rhythm?**

**Example**:
```
Original: "The summer day is beautiful and warm"
Scanned:   The SUM | mer DAY | is BEAU | ti FUL | and WARM
           u   /   | u   /   | u  /    | u  /   | u   /
           ✓ Iambic pentameter (perfect)
```

### Revision Tips
- **Too many syllables?** Remove articles (a, the) or use contractions
- **Too few syllables?** Add descriptive words or expand phrases
- **Wrong stress pattern?** Rearrange word order or choose synonyms
- **Sounds forced?** Rewrite the line - meaning over meter initially

## Step 6: Polish and Refine

### Checklist

**Structure**:
- [ ] 14 lines total
- [ ] ABAB CDCD EFEF GG rhyme scheme
- [ ] Clear volta (turn) at line 9
- [ ] Strong concluding couplet

**Meter**:
- [ ] Each line is iambic pentameter (or close)
- [ ] Rhythm sounds natural when read aloud
- [ ] Strategic variations for emphasis (if any)

**Content**:
- [ ] Clear central idea or argument
- [ ] Vivid imagery and concrete details
- [ ] Logical progression through quatrains
- [ ] Satisfying resolution in couplet

**Language**:
- [ ] No forced rhymes
- [ ] Active, specific verbs
- [ ] Concrete nouns over abstractions
- [ ] Figurative language (metaphor, simile, personification)

**Polish**:
- [ ] Read aloud - does it flow?
- [ ] Every word earns its place
- [ ] No clichés or tired phrases
- [ ] Surprising or fresh perspective

## Example: Completed Sonnet

### Topic: The Power of Memory

```
When fading light recalls the summer's glow,          (A)
And autumn leaves descend in golden rain,             (B)
I walk the paths we walked so long ago,               (A)
And feel your presence in the wind again.             (B)

The bench where once we sat beneath the trees         (C)
Still holds the shape of conversations past,          (D)
And every rustling branch and passing breeze          (C)
Brings back the moments I thought could not last.     (D)

But memory is more than mere recall—                  (E)
It shapes the present with its gentle hand,           (F)
And though you're gone, you're not gone at all:       (E)
Your voice still echoes through this autumn land.     (F)

   So long as I can walk these paths and see,         (G)
   You live in every leaf that falls for me.          (G)
```

### Analysis of Example

**Quatrain 1**: Introduces autumn setting and memory of lost person
**Quatrain 2**: Develops specific memories tied to place
**Quatrain 3**: Volta - "But" shifts to insight about memory's power
**Couplet**: Resolution - memory keeps the person alive

**Rhyme scheme**: ABAB CDCD EFEF GG ✓
**Meter**: Iambic pentameter throughout ✓
**Imagery**: Autumn, leaves, paths, wind, bench ✓
**Turn**: Line 9 - "But memory is more than mere recall" ✓

## Advanced Challenges

Once you've completed a basic sonnet, try these variations:

### 1. Constrained Topic
Write a sonnet about a mundane object (stapler, coffee cup, shoelace) and make it profound.

### 2. Persona Sonnet
Write from the perspective of a historical figure, fictional character, or inanimate object.

### 3. Response Sonnet
Write a sonnet in response to one of Shakespeare's (Sonnet 18, 73, or 130).

### 4. Modern Language
Write a sonnet using contemporary language and references while maintaining the form.

### 5. Metrical Variation
Intentionally break meter at key moments for dramatic effect, then justify your choices.

## Revision Prompts

After completing your first draft, use these prompts to revise:

1. **Imagery**: Can you make any abstract words more concrete?
2. **Verbs**: Can you replace "to be" verbs with active verbs?
3. **Specificity**: Can you replace general words with specific details?
4. **Sound**: Read aloud - where does it stumble? Why?
5. **Surprise**: Does your couplet offer a fresh insight or just repeat?
6. **Necessity**: Does every word earn its place, or are some just filling space?

## Share and Critique

### Peer Review Questions

When sharing with others, ask:
1. Where does the meter feel forced or unnatural?
2. Which images are most vivid? Which are vague?
3. Is the volta (turn) clear and effective?
4. Does the couplet satisfy or surprise?
5. What's the strongest line? The weakest?
