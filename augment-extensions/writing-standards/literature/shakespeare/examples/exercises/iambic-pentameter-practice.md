# Iambic Pentameter Practice

## Overview

This exercise develops your ear for iambic pentameter through progressive practice: listening, scanning, correcting, and creating.

**Time Required**: 45-60 minutes
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate
**Prerequisites**: Basic understanding of stressed/unstressed syllables

## What is Iambic Pentameter?

### The Iamb
An **iamb** is a two-syllable foot with the pattern: **unstressed/stressed** (u /)

**Examples**:
- a**LONE** (u /)
- be**FORE** (u /)
- the **CAT** (u /)
- to **BE** (u /)

### Pentameter
**Pentameter** means five feet (five iambs) per line.

**Pattern**:
```
u / | u / | u / | u / | u /
(10 syllables total)
```

**Example**:
```
Shall I | com PARE | thee TO | a SUM | mer's DAY?
u    /  | u    /   | u   /  | u  /   | u     /
```

**1.** "The beautiful summer day is very warm and nice"

- **Fixed**: "The summer day is warm and beautiful" (10 syllables)
- **Scanned**: The SUM | mer DAY | is WARM | and BEAU | ti FUL

```

**3.** "The moon shines bright tonight"

```

### Answer Key (Exercise 6)

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

**1.** "Never, never, never, never, never!"
- **Variation**: All stressed syllables (5 spondees)
- **Effect**: Extreme emphasis, despair (King Lear)

**2.** "Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May"
- **Variation**: Spondee in foot 1 ("Rough winds")
- **Effect**: Emphasizes violence of wind

**3.** "To be or not to be that is the question"
- **Variation**: Feminine ending (11 syllables)
- **Effect**: Softer ending, contemplative tone

**4.** "The curfew tolls the knell of parting day"
- **Variation**: None - perfect iambic pentameter
- **Effect**: Smooth, regular rhythm suits solemn subject

</details>
