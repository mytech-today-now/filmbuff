## Example: Hamlet Analysis

For a completed example of this worksheet, see how it might be filled out for Hamlet:

**Character Name**: Hamlet
**Play**: Hamlet
**Character Type**: ☑ Protagonist
**Social Status**: ☑ Royalty (Prince of Denmark)

**Dominant Traits**:
1. **Trait**: Intellectual/Philosophical
   - **Evidence**: "To be or not to be" soliloquy (Act 3, Scene 1) - contemplates existence, death, and action

2. **Trait**: Indecisive/Hesitant
   - **Evidence**: Delays killing Claudius despite multiple opportunities; "Thus conscience does make cowards of us all"

3. **Trait**: Melancholic/Depressed
   - **Evidence**: "How weary, stale, flat, and unprofitable / Seem to me all the uses of this world!" (Act 1, Scene 2)

**Character Arc Type**: ☑ Complex Arc (from paralyzed grief to decisive action, but at great cost)

**Tragic Flaw**: Overthinking and inability to act decisively

**Primary Theme**: Revenge vs. Morality, Madness (real vs. feigned), Death and Mortality
